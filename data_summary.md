### Analysis of Andhra Pradesh Government's Farmer Welfare Initiatives (October 2021)

---

#### 1. Document Overview

This document reports on a significant event held on **October 26, 2021, in Amaravati**, where the **Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh, Sri Y.S. Jagan Mohan Reddy (CM Sri Y.S. Jagan)**, digitally disbursed funds directly into the bank accounts of beneficiaries for three major farmer welfare schemes.

The primary **purpose** of the event was to release a total of **Rs. 2190 Crores** for:
1.  **YSR Rythu Bharosa - PM Kisan**
2.  **YSR Sunna Vaddi Panta Runalu (Zero Interest Crop Loans)**
3.  **YSR Yantra Seva Pathakam (Farm Mechanization Scheme)**

In his address, the CM emphasized his government's **farmer-centric approach**, stating that it has fulfilled "100 percent" of its manifesto promises related to farmers. He metaphorically stated that the disbursement was aimed at bringing "Deepavali (Diwali) glow in farmers' eyes a week earlier." The CM also presented a progress report on various agricultural reforms and initiatives undertaken by his government over the past 29 months, highlighting a shift from a past where farmers faced despair to a present where they are empowered.

#### 2. Key Schemes Described

The document details three primary schemes for which funds were disbursed on this day, along with several other significant initiatives and their financial implications.

**A. YSR Rythu Bharosa - PM Kisan (వైయస్ఆర్ రైతుభరోసా-పీఎం కిసాన్)**

*   **Description:** This scheme provides investment support to farmers. The current disbursement is the **second installment for the third consecutive year** of the scheme.
*   **Benefits:** Each eligible farmer family receives an annual financial assistance of **₹13,500**.
*   **Financial Figures/Allocations:**
    *   **Today's Disbursement:** The CM released funds that complete the **₹2052 Crores** allocated for the second installment of the third year. (Of this, **₹972 Crores** was already released in August, meaning **₹1080 Crores** was disbursed today for this specific scheme).
    *   **Cumulative Expenditure:** In the two and a half years (29 months) since the current government took power, a total of **₹18,777 Crores** has been disbursed under the Rythu Bharosa scheme alone.
*   **Eligibility Criteria:** The scheme is inclusive, covering:
    *   Self-cultivating farmers.
    *   Eligible **SC, ST, BC, and Minority tenant farmers**.
    *   Farmers cultivating forest lands.
    *   Farmers cultivating endowment lands.

**B. YSR Sunna Vaddi Panta Runalu (వైయస్ఆర్ సున్నావడ్డీ పంట రుణాలు - YSR Zero Interest Crop Loans)**

*   **Description:** This scheme aims to alleviate the financial burden of interest on crop loans for farmers by reimbursing the interest amount if the loan is repaid on time.
*   **Benefits:** Farmers effectively get interest-free crop loans, as the entire interest amount paid is deposited back into their accounts.
*   **Financial Figures/Allocations:**
    *   **Today's Disbursement:** **₹112.70 Crores** was released today for **6.67 lakh farmers**.
    *   **Cumulative Expenditure:** Since the government assumed power, a total of **₹1674 Crores** has been disbursed under this scheme.
    *   **Arrears Cleared:** This includes **₹1180 Crores** paid by the current government to clear the zero-interest loan arrears left unpaid by the previous government, in addition to **₹382 Crores** disbursed by the current government.
*   **Eligibility Criteria:** Farmers (including tenant farmers) who have taken crop loans up to **₹1 lakh** and repaid them **within one year** (the stipulated timeframe). Eligibility is determined based on e-cropping data.

**C. YSR Yantra Seva Pathakam (వైయస్ఆర్ యంతరసేవా పథకం - YSR Farm Mechanization Scheme)**

*   **Description:** This scheme focuses on enhancing agricultural mechanization by providing modern farm equipment to farmer groups at affordable rents, primarily through Community Hiring Centers.
*   **Benefits:** Farmers gain access to a wide range of farm machinery at reasonable and farmer-determined rental rates, thereby reducing cultivation costs and improving efficiency.
*   **Financial Figures/Allocations:**
    *   **Today's Disbursement:** **₹25.55 Crores** was released today as a subsidy to **1720 farmer groups** for the purchase of agricultural machinery for their Community Hiring Centers.
    *   **Total Project Outlay:** A total of **₹2134 Crores** is being spent statewide to establish **10,750 Community Hiring Centers (CHCs)**, integrated with Rythu Bharosa Kendras (RBKs) at the village level.
    *   **Specialized Centers:** In paddy-rich districts like Godavari, Krishna, and Guntur, an additional **1035 custom hiring centers (CHCs)** equipped with combined harvesters are being made available (5 per mandal).
*   **Target Beneficiaries:** Farmer groups across the state, with 1720 groups directly benefiting from today's subsidy.

#### 3. Key Infrastructure & Delivery Channels

The document highlights significant infrastructure and delivery mechanisms established to ensure effective implementation and reach of agricultural schemes:

*   **Rythu Bharosa Kendras (RBKs - రైతు భరోసా కేంద్రాలు):**
    *   **Number:** 10,778 RBKs have been established across the state.
    *   **Function:** Positioned as "one-stop centers" at the village level, they cater to almost every need of farmers. They provide quality seeds, pesticides, and fertilizers with a government guarantee. Beyond supplies, they offer expert advice and guidance to farmers on all aspects, from "seed to sale."
    *   **Staffing:** Each RBK is staffed by Agricultural Assistants, Animal Husbandry Assistants, and in aquaculture-intensive areas, Aqua Assistants, ensuring specialized support.
    *   **Monitoring & Price Support:** RBKs are integrated with the **"CM App" (Continuous Monitoring of Agricultural Prices and Products)**. If agricultural prices fall within an RBK's jurisdiction, the Agricultural Assistant immediately alerts the Joint Collector and the Marketing Department to initiate market intervention through the Price Stabilization Fund, safeguarding farmers from losses.
    *   **Impact:** The CM asserted that RBKs have brought a "great system" to the village level, guiding farmers every step of the way.

*   **Banking Correspondents (బ్యాంకింగ్ కరస్పాండెంట్స్):**
    *   **Deployment:** 9160 Banking Correspondents have been deployed within RBKs.
    *   **Purpose:** To make banking services, including crop loan transactions, easily accessible to all farmers (including tenant farmers) directly at the RBK level.
    *   **Expansion:** Discussions are ongoing with banks to extend these services to the remaining RBKs in the near future.

*   **Community Hiring Centers (CHCs - కమ్యూనిటీ హైరింగ్ సెంటర్లు):**
    *   **Integration:** 10,750 CHCs are being established and linked to RBKs at the village level.
    *   **Mechanization:** These centers aim to bring agricultural mechanization directly to the grassroots level, making expensive equipment affordable and accessible through rental services. Farmers themselves determine the rental rates.
    *   **Special Focus:** In major paddy-growing districts (Godavari, Krishna, Guntur), 1035 specialized CHCs equipped with combined harvesters are being deployed (5 per mandal).

*   **e-Cropping (ఇ-క్రాపింగ్):**
    *   **Process:** Mandatory registration of every farmer and every crop at RBKs.
    *   **Transparency & Eligibility:** This system ensures transparency and direct identification of beneficiaries for various schemes such as crop insurance, zero-interest loans, and crop procurement, minimizing irregularities.

*   **Agricultural Advisory Councils (వ్యవసాయ సలహా మండళ్ళు):**
    *   **Structure:** Established at four levels: RBK, Mandal, District, and State.
    *   **Meeting Frequency:** Meetings are scheduled regularly – every **first Friday** of the month at RBK level, **second Friday** at Mandal level, and **third Friday** at District level (under the Collector's supervision).
    *   **Function:** These councils provide real-time advice and recommendations on crop planning, farmer assistance, RBK performance improvement, and address farmer issues. Unresolved issues at lower levels are escalated to higher departmental heads and the Agriculture Secretary for resolution.

*   **Marketing Intervention Fund (మార్కెట్ ఇంటర్వెన్షన్ ఫండ్):**
    *   **Purpose:** Created to prevent farmers from incurring losses due to falling market prices. The government intervenes when crops fetch prices below the Minimum Support Price (MSP).
    *   **Example:** Cited intervention in tobacco procurement.

*   **Purity and Quality Control:**
    *   **Focus:** Strong emphasis on preventing adulteration in seeds, pesticides, and fertilizers.
    *   **Government Guarantee:** RBKs supply quality inputs with a direct government guarantee.
    *   **Enforcement:** SPs and Collectors are instructed to conduct stringent inspections and crack down on private sector entities involved in adulteration.

*   **9-hour Daytime Quality Power (పగటిపూటే నాణ్యమైన విద్యుత్):**
    *   **Provision:** Quality, uninterrupted power supply for 9 hours during daytime for agricultural pump sets.
    *   **Beneficiaries:** 18.7 lakh farmers.
    *   **Investment:** Rs. 18,000 Crores spent in 2 years for this purpose.
    *   **Arrears & Modernization:** The government also paid Rs. 10,000 Crores in power bill arrears left by the previous government and spent Rs. 1700 Crores on feeder modernization to ensure quality supply.

*   **YSR Free Crop Insurance (వైయస్ఆర్ ఉచిత పంటల భీమా):**
    *   **Impact:** In 29 months, Rs. 3716 Crores has been provided to 31.07 lakh farmers under this scheme.

*   **Prakruti Vipattula Nidhi (ప్రకృతి వైపరీత్యాల నిధి - Natural Calamities Fund):**
    *   **Allocation:** A fund of Rs. 2000 Crores has been established.

*   **Dharala Sthireekarana Nidhi (ధరల స్థిరీకరణ నిధి - Price Stabilization Fund):**
    *   **Allocation:** A fund of Rs. 3000 Crores has been established to stabilize market prices and procure crops when prices fall below MSP.

*   **Input Subsidy (ఇన్‌పుట్ సబ్సిడీ):**
    *   **New Policy:** A new system has been introduced where input subsidy for crop loss is provided in the *same season* of loss, enabling farmers to use the funds for the subsequent crop.

*   **Aqua Farmers Electricity Subsidy (ఆక్వా రైతులకు కరెంట్ సబ్సిడీ):**
    *   **Benefit:** Electricity is provided at a subsidized rate of **₹1.5 per unit** for aqua farmers.
    *   **Expenditure:** The government spends Rs. 780 Crores annually on this subsidy, totaling Rs. 1560 Crores in two years.

*   **YSR Jala Kala (వైయస్ఆర్ జలకళ):** Free borewells for farmers.

*   **AP Amul Palavelluva (ఏపీ అమూల్ పాలవెల్లువ):** Initiative to support dairy farmers.

*   **MSP for Additional Crops:**
    *   The government ensures MSP for 17 crops announced by the central government and has additionally brought **7 more crops** under MSP, ensuring farmers receive remunerative prices. Posters in RBKs inform farmers about these rates.

*   **Marketing System Reforms:**
    *   **Naadu-Nedu (Then-Now):** Applied to modernize Agricultural Market Committees (AMCs) and establish new agricultural colleges and polytechnics.
